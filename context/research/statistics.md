# Research & Statistics

## The 3.5% Rule
**Source**: Dr. Erica Chenoweth, Harvard Kennedy School
- Analyzed 323 civic movements over 100 years
- No movement with 3.5% active participation has ever failed
- Nonviolent movements succeed 53% of the time (vs 26% violent)
- In Tivon: 3.5% = ~665 residents

## Israeli Civic Engagement
**Source**: Israel Democracy Institute (IDI)
- 71% of Israelis believe protests can create change (2021)
- 57% trust local authorities (vs 46% for national government)
- 49% turnout in 2024 municipal elections
- 46% stopped voting because they don't believe it matters

## Participatory Budgeting
**Source**: Participatory Budget Project
- 11,500+ participatory budgeting processes worldwide
- Average engagement: 3-5% of eligible residents
- Cities that implemented PB saw 20-30% increase in civic trust

## Social Movement Success Factors
**Source**: Various academic studies
- Clear, achievable goals increase success by 40%
- Transparency about process builds 3x more trust
- "Skin in the game" (small financial commitment) increases follow-through by 60%
- Local focus outperforms national campaigns for concrete outcomes
